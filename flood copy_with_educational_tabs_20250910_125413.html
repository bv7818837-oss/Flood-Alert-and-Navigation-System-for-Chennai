Flood Preparedness Guide

Before a Flood

  • Know your area's flood risk and elevation above flood stages
  • Identify nearby bodies of water and their flood potential
  • Learn the elevation level of your property and nearby areas
  • Identify levees and dams in your area and determine if they pose a hazard
  • Sign up for your community's warning system

Create a Plan

  • Plan and practice a flood evacuation route
  • Assemble an emergency supply kit
  • Plan for your pets' safety
  • Keep important documents in a waterproof container
  • Consider flood insurance options

During a Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Follow evacuation orders and don't wait
  • Do not walk through moving water
  • Do not drive into flooded areas
  • Be aware of areas where floodwaters have receded

Maintaining Your Kit

  • Keep canned food in a cool, dry place
  • Store boxed food in tightly closed containers
  • Replace expired items as needed
  • Re-think your needs every year and update your kit as your family's needs change

Basic First Aid for Floods

Cuts and Scrapes

  • Clean the wound with clean, running water
  • Apply pressure to stop bleeding
  • Apply antibiotic ointment
  • Cover with a sterile bandage
  • Watch for signs of infection

CPR Basics

  1. Check for responsiveness and breathing
  2. Call emergency services
  3. Place hands on center of chest
  4. Push hard and fast (100-120 compressions per minute)
  5. Give rescue breaths if trained to do so

Waterborne Illness Prevention

  • Drink only clean, treated water
  • Wash hands frequently with clean water and soap
  • Avoid floodwater contact if you have open wounds
  • Disinfect contaminated surfaces

When to Seek Medical Attention

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest or abdomen
  • Signs of infection (redness, swelling, pus)
  • High fever (above 101¬∞F or 38.3¬∞C)
  • Severe dehydration symptoms
