Emergency First Aid Guide

Essential first aid information for flood-related emergencies. In case of serious injury, call emergency services immediately.

Wound Care

For all wounds, especially in flood conditions, take these precautions:

  • Wash hands thoroughly with clean water and soap before touching wounds
  • Rinse wounds with clean, running water to remove dirt and debris
  • Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth to stop bleeding
  • Apply antibiotic ointment if available
  • Cover with sterile bandage or clean cloth
  • Change dressings daily or when wet/dirty

Seek immediate medical attention for:

  • Deep or large wounds
  • Wounds caused by dirty or rusty objects
  • Animal or human bites
  • Signs of infection (redness, swelling, pus, increasing pain, fever)

Waterborne Illness & Hygiene

Prevent and manage waterborne illnesses:

  • Drink only bottled, boiled, or treated water
  • Wash hands with soap and clean water before eating and after using the toilet
  • Use alcohol-based hand sanitizer if clean water is not available
  • Avoid swallowing flood water
  • Disinfect toys that may be contaminated

Watch for symptoms:

  • Diarrhea or vomiting
  • Stomach cramps
  • Fever
  • Dehydration (dry mouth, dizziness, dark urine)
